Platform Architecture

The execution validation layer between planning and outcomes.

Upstream systems generate intent — schedules, cut lists, specifications. Downstream reality is irreversible — cuts, bends, welds, pours. The platform sits at the point of execution: guiding the action, validating it against the spec, and recording the outcome.

The execution loop

Action-ready workstation instructions

Turn specifications, cut lists, and schedules into clear step-by-step actions. Operators see exactly what to do next — no second-guessing drawings, no manual look-ups.

In-process verification before the action is irreversible

Each step is checked against the live job context, machine profile, and tolerance envelope. Deviations are caught before they turn into scrap, delay, or rework.

Operational traceability from bench to audit

Every decision, measurement, and operator action is logged with timestamps and device context. Your ERP, detailing, and QA systems receive cleaner data on material use, timing, and outcomes.

Built for the shop floor

Infrastructure decisions that reflect how fabrication actually works: intermittent connectivity, dusty environments, shift changes, and compliance audits.

Offline-first architecture

Full execution capability with no network dependency. Data syncs when connectivity returns — no interruption to bench operations.

Edge inference

On-device AI validation runs at the workstation. Decisions happen in milliseconds, not round-trips.

Immutable audit trail

Every event is tamper-evident and append-only. Operator actions, machine readings, and validation outcomes form a continuous, reproducible record.

Versioned machine profiles

Machine calibration data is tracked over time. Drift is visible, and profile changes require controlled release — no silent overrides.

Role-based access

Operators, supervisors, QA, and admins see different surfaces. Governance controls are built in, not bolted on.

Structured exports

Per-run reports, per-job trace logs, exception registries, and calibration summaries export in formats your ERP and QA systems expect.
